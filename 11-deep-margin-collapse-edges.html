Margin collapsing — edge cases

1. Adjacent siblings — larger margin wins

A · margin-bottom 60
B · margin-top 30 — gap is 60, not 90

2. Negative margin — algebraic sum

A · mb 60
B · mt -20 — gap 40 (60-20)

3. Two negatives — most negative wins

A · mb -10
B · mt -30 — overlap by 30

4. Parent-first-child collapse — child's margin escapes

The child's margin-top: 40 escapes the parent and pushes the whole parent down 40px from the previous block.

11. Three-way collapse: parent + first-child + first-grandchild

12. Empty block — margins collapse through it

The empty block in the middle has mt:30, mb:40 — they collapse with each other and with neighbours; total gap = 40 (max of 30, 40, A's 20).
